Standard Form

Standard form is a way of writing numbers that makes it easier to understand and work with very large or very small numbers. It's also known as standard index form or scientific notation.

Formula: A * 10 n

Example:

    * 5,000 = 5 * 103
    * 0.000000002 = 2 * 10-9

Quadratic Equation

The quadratic equation is used to find the solutions (roots) of a second-degree polynomial equation. It is represented as:

Formula: a2 + b2 = c2

Where:
a,b and c are constants
while c is the variable

Arithmetic Progression

Arithmetic Progression (AP) is a sequence of numbers in order that the common difference of any two successive numbers is a constant value. The general form of an Ap is given by: a, a + d, a + 2d, a + 3d

First Term of AP: a + (n-1)d

nth Term of an AP: an= a + (n-1)d

Sum of N Term of AP: sn = n/2[2a + (n-1)d]

Sum of AP when the Last Term is Given.
s = n/2 (first term + last term)
